# 01 — MEIC Strategy Rules

Authoritative business rules for the MEIC (Multiple Entry Iron Condor) trading bot.
Every rule has a stable ID. Code, edge cases (doc 02), and tests (doc 04) reference these IDs.
Any parameter written as `config.<name>` is defined in doc 06 and MUST be configurable — never hardcoded.

**Instrument:** SPX 0DTE options (cash-settled, European exercise, no assignment risk).
**Broker:** tastytrade API. **Market data:** tastytrade DXLink streaming.

---

## 1. Trading day lifecycle (DAY)

- **DAY-01** The bot trades only on days the exchange is open. It MUST consult an exchange calendar (including half days) before scheduling entries.
- **DAY-02** On half days (1:00 PM ET close), any entry scheduled at or after `close_time − config.min_time_before_close` is skipped.
- **DAY-03** The bot operates in ET (America/New_York). All configured times are ET. **Clock verification source (v1.48, operator-ratified — option B):** drift is measured against the **broker's `Date` header** on the authenticated session-probe responses already flowing every ~60 s (NFR) — continuous, no new dependency, no new network path, and measured against the counterparty whose clock governs windows and cutoffs. Resolution is ~1 s, hence the threshold default of 2000 ms. Drift > `config.max_clock_drift_ms` blocks new entries (RSK-07). **Unmeasured = unverified = blocked:** no reading at startup, or the latest reading older than 300 s, counts as failed verification — the pre-flight and every entry gate name `clock_drift` as the reason. If the probe can't reach the broker, blocking entries is correct regardless. Manual env override and NTP were considered and rejected (manual makes the operator a component; NTP adds a blockable dependency for precision nothing here needs — the tightest window is 120 s and resting stops are clock-immune).
- **DAY-04** A trading day has exactly one `TradingDay` record. All entries, orders and fills for the day link to it.
- **DAY-06 Entry-time format & window (v1.53, operator-ratified — ruling captured live 2026-07-09: "within RTH session").** A schedule entry time MUST be 24-hour ("military") ET wall-clock, hour 00–23, minute 00–59 (leading zero optional); colon or dot separator accepted (11.53 ≡ 11:53), persisted canonically as HH:MM. am/pm, compact 0930, unknown separators, out-of-range ⇒ rejected per-row, reason `not_24h_military`. The time MUST satisfy 09:30 ≤ t < 16:00 ET (reason `outside_market_hours`) — in ADDITION to DAY-02's `min_time_before_close` buffer (reason `too_close_to_close`). Backend authoritative; UI checks advisory (UI-03).
- **DAY-05** Mode is either `paper` or `live` (`config.trading_mode`). Mode is fixed for the whole trading day; switching requires no open positions and takes effect next day start.

## 2. Entry schedule (ENT)

- **ENT-01** Entries occur at the times the operator has composed in the UI (`entry_times` + per-entry parameters). **There is no default schedule** (Ash's rule): the shipped configuration contains zero entries. The composed entries form a **standing daily schedule**: while armed, they fire every trading day at their times until the operator changes them. Each defined time is one *entry attempt* per trading day.
- **ENT-01a Arm/Disarm — persistent state (v1.33, Ash's rule).** ARMED/DISARMED is **durable state, not a daily mode**: it is persisted (same durable store as the event log and Stop Trading state) and **restored exactly on every startup** — process restart, crash recovery, Docker container recovery, machine reboot. Arming keeps the system armed **indefinitely, across days**, until the operator disarms; disarming likewise persists until the operator re-arms. There is no daily reset of either state. **DISARMED**: no entries ever fire; open positions remain fully managed (stops, LEX, TPF, DCY, EOD). **ARMED**: the standing schedule fires each trading day, subject to all gates; each new trading day self-initializes without operator action (calendar check DAY-01/02, reconcile, warm-ups). Arming requires ≥ 1 entry composed (arming an empty schedule is rejected) and runs the UC-02 pre-flight once at arm time; the per-day and per-entry gates (ENT-03/08) re-verify everything daily thereafter. Disarming mid-day cancels future entry attempts only — never touches positions or management. A restart mid-day while armed resumes the day: missed windows are skipped (ENT-02), remaining entries fire. An entry fires iff **ARMED ∧ Stop-Trading OFF ∧ Confirm-Live ON** ∧ ENT-03 gates pass.
- **ENT-01b Confirm Live — third persistent gate (v1.34, Ash's rule).** A dedicated UI toggle, one additional deliberate layer of defense before any entry can fire. Independent of Arm and Stop Trading; required in BOTH paper and live modes (uniform gate — paper runs exercise the exact logic live depends on). Durable state like the other two: persisted, restored exactly on every restart including Docker container recovery, never reset by time or boot. Default OFF on fresh install only. Turning it off mid-day blocks future entries and touches nothing else (positions, stops, management all continue). The three trade-enabling states are exactly: ARMED (schedule active), Stop Trading OFF (not halted), Confirm Live ON (deliberate final consent).
- **ENT-02** An entry attempt has a tolerance window of `config.entry_window_seconds` (default 120 s). If the bot cannot begin the attempt within the window (e.g. it was down), that entry is **skipped, never executed late**.
- **ENT-03** Before each entry attempt the bot MUST verify, in order: ARMED (ENT-01a), Confirm Live ON (ENT-01b), Stop Trading not active (RSK-01), no Flatten All executing (RSK-01a), market open and not halted (DAT-04), market data fresh (DAT-02), broker session valid, buying power sufficient for worst-case margin of the new condor. Any failure ⇒ entry skipped and reason logged.
- **ENT-04** Each entry trades its **own per-entry `contracts` count** (v1.44, operator-ratified: range 1–10, default 1) per leg — each row of the standing schedule carries its own size (e.g. 2 MEICs at 10:00, 1 at 11:15). `config.contracts_per_entry` is the pre-fill for new rows only. RSK-04 evaluates the day as the SUM of per-entry worst cases.
- **ENT-05** At most `config.max_entries_per_day` entries may be *filled* per day (default = length of `config.entry_times`). Skipped entries are not retried.
- **ENT-06** Optional filters, each independently toggleable, checked at ENT-03 time: `config.vix_max` (skip if VIX above), `config.skip_dates` (explicit blackout dates, e.g. FOMC), `config.min_total_credit` (see STK-06).
- **ENT-07** Entry attempts are strictly sequential per scheduled time; the bot MUST NOT run two entry attempts concurrently. An attempt still working its order when the next scheduled time arrives is cancelled first (ORD-06).
- **ENT-08 Pre-entry session warm-up.** At `config.session_warmup_lead_seconds` (default 60 s) before each scheduled entry time, the bot runs a warm-up probe so the entry never spends its window on auth or cold connections:
  1. Validate the tastytrade session token with a lightweight authenticated call; renew proactively if invalid or expiring within `config.session_token_expiry_buffer_seconds` (default 300 s) — per REC-06.
  2. Verify the account order stream heartbeat is live; reconnect if not.
  3. Verify DXLink subscriptions for the SPXW chain are live and ticking, so quotes are already fresh (STK-04) at fire time; resubscribe if not.
  4. On failure: retry with backoff until the entry time; raise an alert if still failing at T−10 s. The warm-up itself never delays or blocks the scheduled entry — at fire time the ENT-03 gates decide as normal (unresolved session ⇒ skip, reason `invalid_session`).
  5. If the bot starts inside the warm-up window (e.g. T−30 s), the probe runs immediately, compressed.

## 3. Strike selection and condor construction (STK)

- **STK-01** Each condor has 4 legs on the same expiration (today): short put, long put (lower), short call, long call (higher).
- **STK-02** Short strike selection method is `config.strike_method`:
  - `premium` (default — Ash's probe walk, v1.39, supersedes the v1.4/1.5 ceiling walk): round every candidate strike's mid to the nearest $0.05 (its "probe price"). Starting at `target_premium` T, probe in this exact order: T, T−0.05, T+0.05, T−0.10, T+0.10, T−0.15, T+0.15, then DOWN ONLY in $0.05 steps. Hard limits: at most `config.probe_up_max` (default 3) probes above target (cap T+0.15) and at most `config.probe_down_max` (default 25) probes below (floor T−1.25); the effective floor is max(T−1.25, `min_short_premium`) — never sell a short under $1.00 regardless of depth. The FIRST probe that matches any strike's probe price wins; if two strikes round to the same probe price, take the one whose raw mid is closest to the probe, ties further OTM. All probes exhausted ⇒ skip `no_valid_strikes`. Example, target $3.00: chain offering 3.45 / 3.20 / 2.80 sells the 2.80 strike — 3.20 is equally near the target but beyond the 3-up cap, so it is NEVER selected.
  - `delta`: choose the strike whose absolute delta is closest to `config.short_delta_target` (default 0.10) without exceeding `config.short_delta_max` (default 0.15).
- **STK-02a Net credit is a consequence, not a target.** Because selection targets the short leg, net side credit (short mid − long mid) **fluctuates with the cost of the wing**: a $3.00 short put with a $1.00 long nets $2.00; later in the day the same $3.00 short may carry a $2.00 long and net only $1.00. The system MUST maintain this distinction everywhere:
  - Strike selection uses `target_premium` (short leg).
  - Gate bases differ by design (v1.29): STK-05 floors the **short leg's gross premium** per side; STK-06 and the entry-order floor ORD-03 evaluate the **total NET credit with longs factored** — total net < $2.00 aborts the trade (reason `insufficient_credit`). The total-net floor is the defense against expensive wings.
  - All stop math (STP-02, both bases) and all P&L use **actual net fill credits**, never `target_premium`.
  - The UI labels the two numbers distinctly: "short premium" vs "net credit" (UI-14).
- **STK-03** Long strikes are `config.wing_width` points (default 50) beyond the shorts.
- **STK-04** Strike/delta decisions use streaming greeks and quotes no older than `config.max_quote_age_ms` (default 3000 ms). Stale data ⇒ entry attempt aborted (DAT-02).
- **STK-05** Per-side minimum SHORT premium (Ash's basis, v1.29): each SHORT leg's mid must be ≥ `config.min_short_premium` (default $1.00) — the gross premium collected per short, wings NOT factored. If either short fails, the entire entry is skipped. (Deliberate consequence, accepted by Ash: a side may net very little after an expensive wing and still trade, provided the TOTAL net floor (STK-06) passes — the total is the binding economic gate.) **Single-side (spread-only) entries are prohibited** — the condor is always traded whole (ORD-01).
- **STK-06** Total condor credit at mid must be ≥ `config.min_total_credit` (default $2.00), else skip the entry.
- **STK-07** All computed strikes MUST exist in the actual chain with non-zero bid on the shorts. Otherwise the entry is skipped.
- **STK-08** Prices are rounded to the instrument's tick (SPX: $0.05 below $3.00, $0.10 at/above — obtain tick rules from the API, don't hardcode).
- **STK-10 Chain-integrity gate — TRADE-RELATIVE (v1.51, operator-ratified; supersedes the fixed ATM band, retired after two mid-session retunes on 2026-07-09).** Before any strike selection, the gate inspects exactly the strikes THIS entry can reach, computed per side from the entry's own parameters: every strike whose rounded mid the probe walk could match (target premium T, up to `probe_up_max` above, down to the T−1.25/`min_short_premium` floor), PLUS the wing strike at each reachable short (wing width), PLUS the STK-09 shift budgets (short: +2 OTM shifts with wing; long: +5 solo shifts). At least `config.chain_completeness_pct` (default 90%, NOW WIRED — hardcoding it was a defect) of that reachable set MUST have valid two-sided marks, evaluated per option type; the specific strikes ultimately selected are additionally 100%-fresh per STK-11. Rationale: a FIXED band cannot track the moving far-OTM dead-strike boundary (listed-but-never-quoted 0DTE strikes move with spot/vol) — the gate failed at ±120 and again at ±70 within one session while every reachable strike was perfectly quoted; measuring the trade's own shopping range is immune by construction and also catches a dead LONG wing upfront (previously slipped through to fail later as wing_unmarked). Staleness (STK-04) is not completeness — a fresh-but-holey chain (typical right after a broker re-login) is exactly the condition that mis-selects strikes. On failure: retry every `config.chain_retry_seconds` (default 5 s) within the entry window (ENT-02); if the window expires unhealed, skip the entry, reason `incomplete_chain`. `chain_atm_band_pts` is RETIRED (config key rejected). **Baseline pre-validation (v1.55, operator-designed — completes the trade-relative rework):** at ENT-08 warm-up (T−60s; manual ENT-09 entries: at press), capture the entry's **validated universe** = the reachable strikes holding fresh two-sided quotes at that moment, logged deterministically (day report). Thereafter: (1) fire-time completeness is `chain_completeness_pct` of the VALIDATED universe still fresh — listed-but-dead-at-baseline strikes were never part of the tradeable picture and count against NOTHING; the gate measures REGRESSION from a known-good picture, which is what feed health means; (2) selection shops only inside the validated universe — a candidate whose wing lands on an unvalidated strike is skipped as an infeasible candidate (walk/shift continues), never a whole-entry failure; (3) **viability floor**: the universe must hold ≥ `config.min_validated_strikes` (default 10) candidates per side, else alert at warm-up (60s of early warning) and retry within the window, skipping `incomplete_chain` if unhealed — a sick-at-baseline feed must never trivially pass by validating a sliver. Sick-feed guards unchanged (DAT-02, STK-04, STK-11).
- **STK-11 Probe-match integrity (v1.39, reworked for the probe walk).** The selected strike's RAW mid must be within $0.025 of its matched probe price (true by construction of nearest-0.05 rounding — asserted as a sanity invariant), its quote fresh per STK-04 at selection time, and the probe sequence deterministic and logged (the day report shows which probe number matched). A missing/unmarked wing strike (STK-07) retries within the entry window before skipping. The STK-10 completeness gate remains the primary defense against holey chains.
- **STK-09 Strike collision avoidance (Ash's rules, ratified).** SPX options net per symbol; opposite-type orders at an occupied strike destructively merge — selling where a long exists closes that hedge; buying where a short exists strips a stop-protected short. Same-type merges are additive and harmless. Hence:
  - **Same type stacks — BOT-OWNED lots only.** A new short at a strike already holding a BOT short, or a new long at a strike already holding a BOT long, is permitted — no shift. Per-entry attribution remains exact via order-ID fills (OWN-01); the reconciliation blur on shared strikes is accepted. **FOREIGN-occupied strikes block BOTH types (v1.49):** stacking onto a foreign lot would be bot-initiated symbol-sharing, which OWN-08 forbids — a foreign-occupied strike is treated as blocked regardless of type, and the normal shift budgets apply (exhausted ⇒ skip `strike_collision`). Foreign working orders count as occupancy.
  - **Opposite type blocks.** A short is blocked by an existing LONG at its target; a long is blocked by an existing SHORT at its target. For these checks, "existing" includes **in-flight working orders** referencing the strike (races caught before piles exist); same-type in-flight orders do not block.
  - **Short shift budget — 3 strikes total.** If the short's target is blocked: shift one strike further OTM (puts: down; calls: up), wing moving with it (width preserved). Blocked again: shift once more. Blocked at all three strikes (original + `config.max_strike_shifts` = 2 shifts): **abort the entire entry**, reason `strike_collision`. Sides are judged independently.
  - **Long shift budget — 5 strikes, independent.** If the short placed but the long's target is blocked (by a short): shift the long ALONE further OTM, up to `config.max_long_shifts` (default 5) times, until a free or long-occupied strike is found. **Each solo long shift widens the spread** — worst case per spread = (widened width − credit); RSK-04's max-risk gate evaluates the widened width and may veto the entry. If 5 shifts don't find a home: abort the entry (`strike_collision`) — never a naked short, never single-side.
  - After all shifts, the credit gates (STK-05/06) re-run on the final strikes; the premium ceiling (STK-02) holds by construction since shifts move OTM only.

## 4. Entry order execution (ORD)

- **ENT-09 Manual entry (v1.44, operator-ratified).** The operator may fire an entry on demand from the UI (▶), outside any scheduled window — the ONLY rule it bypasses is the ENT-02 window (which guards against stale scheduled intent; a manual fire is fresh intent by definition). Everything else applies unreduced: the FULL ENT-03 gate chain (armed ∧ Confirm Live ∧ Stop Trading off ∧ no flatten executing ∧ market open ∧ data fresh ∧ session valid ∧ buying power ∧ order cap ∧ RSK-04), reconcile-block and clock-drift checks, and ENT-07 sequencing (never concurrent with another attempt). It counts toward `max_entries_per_day`. Confirmation: an OK-confirmation dialog (operator-ratified: simple OK, not typed) MUST be acknowledged before the order is submitted, in BOTH paper and live mode, showing the entry's parameters and computed structural worst case (UI-22). Idempotent per press: a double-click produces exactly one attempt. Recorded with initiator `manual_entry`, tagged like manual actions in reports (UC-08 tagging). A gate failure is shown with its reason exactly as a scheduled skip.
- **ENT-10 Arm runs the day (v1.53, operator-ratified; live finding 2026-07-09 — armed with a composed 12:35 entry, nothing watched the clock).** Entering ARMED MUST start the scheduled trading day: a runtime task watches the wall clock and attempts each composed entry at its time. No separate "start" action may exist. (1) **Arm ⇒ watching**: on successful Arm (pre-flight passed) the day task starts for entries whose time is still in the future; already-passed times are not attempted (ENT-02 guards borderline cases). (2) **Boot restore**: booting with persisted ARMED starts the task automatically (REC-07) — a restart must never turn a watching bot inert. (3) **Disarm ⇒ not watching**: future entries stop immediately; an in-flight attempt is ATOMIC — completes or cancels cleanly, never abandoned mid-flight. (4) **Entry identity is DURABLE (operator ruling, extends v1.47 pin-at-Save)**: every schedule row receives a durable entry id at Save; the day task, ORD-04 idempotency keys, exposure book, and attempted-today tracking key on the durable id, NEVER on list position — mid-day edits (add/delete/reorder) while ARMED remain permitted and can never renumber, silently drop, or double-fire an entry. Blocking saves while armed was REJECTED (breaks "editable until each fires"; adds a forget-to-re-arm hazard). (5) **Every rail applies** at fire time (ENT-03, RSK-04/08, STP-02c); an entry already attempted today is never re-attempted. (6) **A crashed day task alerts and stays down** (RSK-06 critical) until the operator cycles Disarm→Arm — never an auto-restart loop placing orders (predecessor's retry-spam lesson).
- **ENT-09b Manual minimum short-strike floors (v1.57, operator-ratified — manual fires ONLY).** The ▶ dialog gains a "minimum strikes" toggle (default OFF; dialog unchanged until enabled). When on: two INDEPENDENTLY OPTIONAL dropdowns (put side, call side — set either, both, or one) populated from the entry's live VALIDATED UNIVERSE (v1.55 — a dead strike can never be selected), puts descending / calls ascending from the money, ≥ 10 strikes shown each side and scrollable deeper; each row displays strike, distance from spot in points, and live mid; spot + quote timestamp in the dialog header. **Semantics = FLOOR, never a pin**: puts select short ≤ chosen strike, calls select short ≥ chosen strike; the probe walk runs unchanged among qualifying strikes (up-probes filtered by the floor; collision shifts only move outward and cannot violate it). Credit rules are UNTOUCHED: if the floor makes STK-05 ($1 gross) / STK-06 ($2 net) / STP-02c jointly unsatisfiable, the fire skips `no_valid_strikes` — the floor can only cause a skip, never weaken a credit rule. An advisory preview line shows the best qualifying premium beyond the floor before OK. **Refuse-and-re-pick (operator-ratified)**: at OK, if spot has crossed a selected floor (call floor ≤ spot, or put floor ≥ spot), the fire is REFUSED with reason `floor_inside_spot` and the operator re-selects — the bot never silently reinterprets an expired instruction. Floors are per-press only (never schedule state), recorded in the entry's events and day report ("manual fire, put floor 7450 / call floor 7500"); the entry's STK-10 reachable set and baseline begin at the floor.
- **ORD-01** The condor is submitted as a **single 4-leg limit order**, never as two separate spreads and never legged in. All-or-nothing per contract: legs fill balanced as a complex order. An unbalanced-leg position must therefore never arise from entry; if reconciliation ever finds one, it is flattened immediately (EC-ENT-06).
- **ORD-02** Initial limit price = mid credit. If unfilled after `config.entry_reprice_seconds` (default 20 s), reprice down one tick. Repeat up to `config.entry_reprice_attempts` (default 5) times.
- **ORD-03** The limit price must never go below `config.min_total_credit` (net, longs factored). Reaching the floor unfilled ⇒ cancel and skip the entry.
- **ORD-04** Every order carries a client-generated idempotency key (deterministic from day + entry number + intent). Before submitting, the bot MUST check for an existing order with the same key (prevents duplicates after crash/restart, see REC rules).
- **ORD-05** Order state is driven by broker fill/status events, never assumed. An order is `filled` only on a broker fill confirmation for full quantity.
- **ORD-06** If the entry order is still working when the attempt's window closes or the next entry time arrives: cancel, confirm cancellation, and handle any partial fill per EDGE-ORD-* rules in doc 02 (summary: a partially filled condor MUST be completed or flattened immediately; never left as an unintended naked or lopsided position).
- **ORD-07** All exit-side orders (stops, long sales, flattening) are subject to the same idempotency and event-driven state rules.
- **ORD-08 Cancel-failure taxonomy.** Every failed cancel MUST be classified before any retry decision, into exactly one of: **(a) filled** — the order executed while the cancel was in flight ⇒ handle as a fill (EC-API-06); **(b) terminal** — the order no longer exists (expired, already cancelled, rejected-dead) ⇒ mark dead, stop tracking, NEVER retry the cancel, reconcile state to broker truth; **(c) transient** — the request failed (timeout, rate limit, maintenance) but the order is likely alive ⇒ bounded retry with backoff. An unclassifiable failure defaults to transient with a hard retry cap and an escalation alert. This applies to every cancel in the system: stop management, entry-order cancels, CLS, LEX, TPF, DCY. Rationale: treating "order doesn't exist" as retryable produced an infinite all-night retry loop in the predecessor system.

## 5. Stop-loss placement on shorts (STP)

The defining rule set of this system: **stops rest at the broker/exchange on the short legs only**, so protection survives bot, network or machine failure.

- **ORD-09 Broker-truth leg identity (v1.45, operator-ratified — Option A).** Every fill event MUST record, per leg, the **broker-reported instrument symbol** (OCC) and the **broker-allocated fill price**, exactly as returned by the broker. ALL subsequent order actions on those legs — stop placement (STP-01), LEX, DCY buybacks, CLS closes, flatten, watchdog escalations — MUST use the recorded symbol; leg identity is NEVER reconstructed from strikes/expiry/right at action time (reconstruction re-runs symbology math on every use — the same drift class as the intent-translation defect). Reconstruction is permitted only as a cross-check that alerts on mismatch, never as the source. The recorded allocations are the data source for STP-02d reconciliation records and the OWN fill-derived ledger; recording is identical in paper (simulator-assigned symbols) and live.

- **STP-01** Immediately upon condor fill confirmation, the bot places two stop orders (buy-to-close), one per short leg, each for the full entry quantity, TIF = Day (0DTE — orders die with the session; EOD-03 still verifies nothing is left working). **Quantity invariant (v1.45, operator-ratified):** each stop's quantity MUST equal its short leg's filled quantity (the entry's per-entry `contracts`) — a stop protecting less than the position is silent nakedness. Any working stop discovered with a mismatched quantity — at placement confirmation, on reconcile, or at any later check — is an **UNPROTECTED condition handled per STP-04** (and OWN-10 rules apply if the mismatch stems from an operator resize).
- **STP-02** Stop trigger price per short is computed per `config.stop_basis`, rounded **DOWN (floor) to the legal tick** per STK-08 — floor rounding is contract-preserving: the both-sides loss can never exceed the promise by a rounding accident (Ash-ratified, v1.39). All modes use **actual fill prices**.
  - `total_credit` (DEFAULT — Ash's ratified outcome contract, v1.38): trigger = `stop_loss_pct` × total NET condor credit, one absolute price level shared by both shorts. E.g. net credit $4.00 at 95% ⇒ both stops at $3.80. **The outcome contract this buys:** one side stopped while the other survives ⇒ small NET PROFIT ≈ (1 − pct) × credit plus long recovery (banked 4.00, paid 3.80 ⇒ +$20 minimum); both sides stopped ⇒ NET LOSS ≈ (2·pct − 1) × credit, never more before slippage (4.00 − 7.60 ⇒ −$360 ≈ the premium). The winning side's credit is the hedge; wing costs cancel out of the math. Trade-off, accepted: triggers sit much closer to the fills, so small wobbles stop out more often.
  - `short_premium` (Rob's formula, available per entry): trigger = short leg fill × (1 + `stop_loss_pct`). Per-leg caps (each short risks pct of its own premium), wider triggers, fewer noise stop-outs — but one-sided hits lose money and whipsaws can exceed the credit. Uses only the stopped leg's own fill.

  - `per_side`: trigger = short leg fill price + (that side's net credit × `stop_loss_pct`), where side net credit = short fill − long fill. Each side managed on its own credit — e.g. short put filled $3.00, long put $0.75 ⇒ side credit $2.25; at 95% trigger = 3.00 + 2.1375 = $5.14 (rounded to tick).
  - `stop_loss_pct` default = 95%; UI-selectable from 95% to 300% in 5% increments (UI-04). The valid set is exactly {95, 100, 105, …, 300}.
  - **Leg-price allocation caveat (per_side mode only — `short_premium` and `total_credit` are unaffected):** a 4-leg complex order fills at a net price; per-leg fill prices are broker allocations of that net and may not match true leg marks. `per_side` math uses the allocated leg fills. Sandbox verification was performed in Phase 2 (STP-05a run) and FAILED to reconcile — allocated legs summed to $0.50 on a $0.05 net fill, one leg at $0.00. Consequently `per_side` is GATED per STP-02d until real-fill verification.
  - Changing `stop_loss_pct` or `stop_basis` intraday applies to **subsequent entries only**; existing resting stops are not modified unless the operator explicitly uses UC-08 (doc 03).
- **STP-02b Manual rebate markup.** `config.stop_rebate_markup` (default $0.00; $0.00–$5.00 in $0.05 steps; per-entry overridable; UI-selectable) is **added to the stop trigger price** computed by STP-02, in both bases, before tick rounding:
  - `per_side`: trigger = short fill + (side credit × pct) + markup.
  - `total_credit`: trigger = (total credit × pct) + markup.
  - Purpose: while NLE calibration data accumulates, the operator can manually pre-credit the long's expected recovery so the realized **net** loss lands nearer the intended percentage. This is an operator-set constant — it is NOT model-driven; NLE-04's prohibition on automatic trigger adjustment stands.
  - Consequence the UI MUST display when markup > 0 (UI-18): worst case rises by the markup — if the long recovers less than the markup, net loss exceeds the nominal percentage by the shortfall.
  - NLE estimates (NLE-01) use the markup-inclusive trigger automatically; every calibration record (NLE-06) stores the markup in force, so realized recoveries can be compared against it and the markup retired or tuned as data accumulates.
  - Intraday changes apply to subsequent entries only (as STP-02); UC-08 to modify an existing stop.
- **STP-02c Trigger feasibility guard (v1.38 — required by the total_credit default).** A trigger at or below a short's own price is not a stop, it is an instant exit. Two checkpoints: (1) **pre-entry**, during selection: the estimated trigger (chosen basis on estimated net credit, plus markup) must exceed each short's mid by at least `config.min_stop_distance_ticks` (default 2); otherwise skip the entry, reason `infeasible_stop` — thin-credit condors whose math cannot honor the contract are never opened. (2) **post-fill**, on actual fills: if the recomputed trigger is not above a short's fill by the minimum distance (rare — fills are ladder-bounded), do NOT place a stop that would fire at birth; close the entry immediately via the canonical close (CLS-01, initiator `infeasible_stop`) and alert.
- **STP-02d per_side allocation gate (v1.43, operator-ratified).** The cert sandbox showed broker per-leg allocations that do not reconcile to the net fill (Phase 2 finding), and `per_side` triggers are computed entirely from those allocations — an unverified input feeding stop math is a silent-drift risk, so it is made impossible rather than merely detectable. (1) **Gate:** config validation REJECTS `stop_basis = per_side` (reason `allocation_unverified`), globally and per-entry; `total_credit` and `short_premium` are unaffected. There is NO runtime toggle — the gate is spec-level and lifting it requires an operator-ratified spec amendment. (2) **Passive evidence:** on EVERY real (non-paper) condor fill, regardless of basis, the adapter logs an allocation-reconciliation record: Σ(allocated leg prices) vs net fill price (PASS iff within one tick and no zero-priced leg that did not actually trade at zero). Evidence accumulates from normal trading; nobody trades to test. (3) **Ungate criterion, fixed now:** 5 consecutive PASSED records from real fills. Fewer, or any FAIL resetting the streak, and the gate stands. The per_side formulas (STP-02, STP-02b, TC-STP-01/02/14/16) remain in the spec and the domain — only selection is blocked. The adapter's ACL surfaces raw allocations for PNL-04 in all bases regardless of this gate.
- **STP-03** Stop order type is `config.stop_order_type` = `stop_market` (default, recommended). Rationale: a stop-limit can go unfilled in a gap, leaving a naked short SPX option in a runaway market with no guarantee the bot is alive. If `stop_limit` is configured, an unfilled-stop watchdog is mandatory: if triggered and unfilled after `config.stop_limit_escalation_seconds` (default 10 s), the bot cancels and replaces with a market order; if the bot is down, this protection does not exist — the UI must display this warning when `stop_limit` is selected.
- **STP-03b Stop watchdog — secondary trigger layer (v1.41, ruling on the STP-05a item-2 indeterminate verdict).** The bot monitors each tracked short's mark via the QuoteHub. If the mark sits at or above the resting stop's trigger for `config.watchdog_grace_seconds` (default 10) with the resting stop unfilled: critical alert. If still unfilled at `config.watchdog_escalate_seconds` (default 20, total from first breach): the bot fires its own buy-to-close (marketable limit at the ask) and cancels the resting stop — ORD-08 taxonomy governs the race, a stop filling mid-escalation wins and the escalation aborts; no leg is ever bought twice. The watchdog SUPPLEMENTS the resting stop, never replaces it: broker-side protection remains primary and bot-independent; the watchdog exists solely for the case where the broker's trigger source (unconfirmed in cert — item 2) proves slower than the mark. Escalations log initiator `watchdog_escalation`, count in calibration (each firing is empirical evidence about the broker's true trigger source), and route the side into the normal SIDE_STOPPED → LEX path. Stale marks pause the watchdog clock (DAT-02); the watchdog is day-scoped and event-driven like all monitors.
- **STP-04** Placement of both stops MUST be verified (working-order confirmation from broker). A condor with any unconfirmed stop is in state `UNPROTECTED` — the bot retries placement every `config.stop_retry_seconds` up to `config.stop_retry_attempts`, then flattens that side (or whole condor per `config.unprotected_action`) and raises a critical alert. A position is never knowingly left without a resting stop for more than `stop_retry_seconds × stop_retry_attempts`.
- **STP-05** The bot MUST verify (and test — see TC-STP-08) that stops are held broker-side as working orders visible via the API independent of the bot's own session, i.e. they persist if the bot disconnects. This is the design intent behind shorts-only stops; whether they rest at CBOE natively or are broker-simulated, they MUST NOT depend on the bot process being alive. Wording discipline: say **broker-resting**, not "exchange-resting", until proven otherwise.
- **STP-05a Trigger source.** Before any other build work, verify what price triggers a tastytrade stop on a single SPXW option: last trade vs NBBO/mark. Last-trade triggering is dangerous on sparsely traded strikes (late/no trigger in a fast move; false trigger on an off-market print). If the trigger source is last-trade-only or single-leg option stops are unsupported, STOP and escalate to the operator with a design amendment proposal (e.g. bot-side watchdog as a secondary trigger layer) — do not improvise a workaround.
- **STP-06** No stops are placed on long legs. Longs are managed by the LEX rules only.
- **STP-07** When a short's stop fills, that side enters state `SIDE_STOPPED`; the realized loss on the short and the pending long sale are attributed to that entry.
- **STP-08** The other side's stop remains untouched when one side stops. Both sides of one condor can stop in the same day (whipsaw) — this is normal and must be handled, not treated as an error.
- **STP-09** There is no profit target. Untouched sides are held to expiration and cash-settle (EOD-01), unless `config.eod_close_time` is set (EOD-02).

## 5a. Net-loss estimation (NLE) — informational only

A single-leg resting stop caps the short-leg loss deterministically, but the realized *net* side loss is reduced by a path-dependent long rebate (fast early move ⇒ large rebate; slow late grind ⇒ near-zero or negative; call side systematically smaller than put side due to vol-spot correlation). The bot therefore computes a **chain-implied net-loss estimate** for transparency and calibration. **It never influences trigger math.**

- **NLE-01 Computation.** At each condor fill, per side:
  1. Implied move D: find, by interpolation on the live chain (same side, same expiry), the strike K* whose option mid equals that side's stop trigger price; D = |K* − short strike|.
  2. Raw long estimate: interpolated current mid of the option at (long strike shifted D closer to the money).
  3. Haircut for elapsed-time decay: estimate = raw × (1 − `config.nle_haircut_pct`), default 30%.
  4. Estimated net loss = (trigger − short fill) − (estimate − long fill), reported in $ and as % of the stop-basis credit.
- **NLE-02 Per side, always.** Put and call estimates are computed and displayed independently; no blended number.
- **NLE-03 Degrades silently.** Stale data (DAT-02) or insufficient strikes to interpolate ⇒ estimate = UNAVAILABLE. NLE can never block, delay, or alter an entry or a stop.
- **NLE-04 Structural isolation.** The estimator lives in a module with no write path to order placement. Stop triggers MUST be byte-identical whether NLE is enabled, disabled, or failing.
- **NLE-05 UI surfacing.** Entry cards show "nominal {pct}% ≈ est. net {y}% (put) / {z}% (call)". The `stop_loss_pct` selector shows a live preview using the current chain when the market is open (UI-13).
- **NLE-06 Calibration log.** Every short-stop event records: entry id, side, timestamp, trigger, stop fill price and slippage, long fill at entry, long sale proceeds, realized net loss ($ and %), the NLE estimate made at entry, and estimate error. Included in the EOD-05 report.
- **NLE-07 Calibration view.** Once ≥ `config.nle_min_samples` (default 25) stop events exist, reports show realized average net-loss ratio and estimate error per side, to guide `stop_loss_pct` selection. Below the sample threshold the view states "insufficient data" — no extrapolation from small samples. Still informational only; any automatic trigger adjustment is a future spec amendment, not an implementation decision.

## 6. Long leg exit after short stop (LEX)

When a short is stopped out, the corresponding long has appreciated. It is sold via a **dynamic reprice ladder** priced off the live chain — never a fixed markup.

- **LEX-01** Trigger: broker fill event for a short-leg stop. The bot immediately begins the long-sale procedure for that side's long leg. Target latency from stop-fill event to first sell order: ≤ `config.lex_start_latency_ms` (default 2000 ms).
- **LEX-02** Quote validation before pricing: the long's quote must be no older than `config.max_quote_age_ms`, not crossed (bid > ask), and have spread ≤ `config.lex_max_spread_ticks`. Invalid quote ⇒ skip to LEX-05 fallback after `config.lex_quote_wait_seconds`.
- **LEX-03** Ladder: place limit sell at mid. If unfilled after `config.lex_reprice_seconds` (default 15 s), cancel/replace one tick lower. Repeat up to `config.lex_reprice_attempts` (default 4) times.
- **LEX-04** Price floor: never place a sell below max(current bid, intrinsic value). Intrinsic = max(0, strike − spot) for puts, max(0, spot − strike) for calls.
- **LEX-05** Fallback: if the ladder is exhausted, or quotes were unusable at LEX-02, place a marketable limit at the current bid (re-fetched). Never a raw market order.
- **LEX-06** If the fallback also fails to fill within `config.lex_fallback_timeout_seconds` (default 30 s), raise a critical alert and keep retrying the fallback loop; the long is a decaying asset and the operator must be notified. (A long-only residual is defined-risk; this is an income leak, not a blow-up risk.)
- **LEX-07** The long is **always sold** — there is no keep-cheap-longs threshold. Every stopped side fully closes; per-side position state after LEX completes is flat.
- **LEX-08** Race handling: any fill that arrives during a cancel/replace supersedes the ladder (the position is what the broker says it is). Cancel/replace must use the broker's atomic replace where available; otherwise confirm-cancel-before-replace.
- **LEX-09** If a fill for the long sale arrives after the bot considered the order cancelled (late fill), reconcile to broker truth (REC-02) and adjust P&L records.

## 6a. Canonical close procedure (CLS)

There is exactly **one** close procedure in the system. Every path that closes an entry — the UI "Close trade" button, a take-profit floor trigger, Flatten All, EOD close — invokes the same implementation, differing only in the recorded initiator.

- **CLS-01 Procedure (v1.50, operator-ratified REPLACE-BASED close — supersedes cancel-first; born from the 2026-07-09 live incident debate).** For a given entry: (1) for EACH short leg with a resting stop: **cancel/replace the stop with a marketable buy-to-close limit** (same leg, full ledger quantity, aggressive cap per LEX fallback rules) — the protection *becomes* the exit in one broker operation: there is never a moment without a working buy order on the short (zero naked seconds) and never a moment with two (zero double-fill race); (2) if the replace returns FILLED (ORD-08a — the stop executed first), that side is already closed: route to SIDE_STOPPED + LEX as before; if the replace fails transient, the ORIGINAL STOP STILL RESTS — the position was never unprotected; retry per ORD-08; (3) close remaining long legs via the LEX-style ladder (plain sells; longs carry no stops and no race); a short with NO resting stop (never confirmed, USER_UNPROTECTED, etc.) is closed with a direct marketable buy-to-close; (4) nothing of the entry is left resting or open when CLS completes (except positions expiring per EOD-01); orphaned artifacts found later are cancelled per reconcile; (5) all orders carry idempotency keys (ORD-04) — no leg is ever closed twice. Rationale recorded: cancel-first accepted ~seconds of nakedness; close-first (rejected) armed a two-orders-one-leg race that can silently double-buy into an unintended long; replace achieves both goals.
- **CLS-02 Single implementation, enforced structurally.** One application service (`CloseEntry(entry_id, initiator)`, doc 05) is the only code path that performs CLS-01. **Binding rule (v1.50, operator-ratified after the agent's hand-rolled close incident): EVERY close — bot, panel, or ANY agent/tooling acting on the system's behalf — routes through `CloseEntry`. No ad-hoc broker-side orders by an agent, ever; an agent that cannot use `CloseEntry` asks the operator instead of improvising.** (The OPERATOR's own right to act directly at the broker is untouched — OWN-09/10/11 govern, bot stands down.) Initiators: `manual` (single-entry close, UC-14), `manual_flatten` (flatten all, RSK-01a), `take_profit` (TPF floor), `take_profit_target` (TPT, v1.58), `eod`, `decay` (short-only scope, DCY-02), `infeasible_stop` (STP-02c post-fill). (No `kill_switch` initiator exists: Stop Trading never closes anything.) A test MUST assert that a manual close and a TPF-triggered close of identical positions produce identical order sequences (TC-CLS-01).
- **CLS-03 Pre-fill entries.** If the entry's opening order is still PENDING/WORKING (nothing filled), "close" means cancel the entry order and resolve any partial per EC-ENT-06 — no close orders are placed for unfilled legs.
- **CLS-05 Boundary with the operator-override rule.** OWN-09/10/11's zero-touch principle applies to interventions made **directly at the broker** (tastytrade app/platform). Commands issued through the bot's own UI (Close trade, Flatten all, Re-protect, Resize/Cancel-stop buttons) are commands TO the bot — they are bot actions with full order cleanup included: stops cancelled, legs closed, nothing orphaned. A bot-commanded close never leaves a resting order behind.
- **CLS-04 Recording.** Every close is evented with initiator, per-leg prices, and resulting P&L; the day report distinguishes which mechanism closed each entry.

## 6b. Take-profit floor (TPF) — operator-set, bot-monitored

An optional per-entry profit floor set by the operator from the UI. When the entry's profit falls back to the floor, the bot closes the remaining position, locking the gain. This complements STP-09 (no automatic profit target): the *bot* never sets one; the *operator* may.

- **TPF-01 Definition.** Floor is a percentage of the entry's **total net credit**. Entry profit% = (realized P&L of closed sides + unrealized P&L of open sides at mid) / total net credit. Trigger: profit% ≤ floor. Example: credit $4.00, floor 20% ⇒ close when profit falls to ≤ $0.80.
- **TPF-02 Selectable levels.** The level set is {5, 10, …, 90}. A level is selectable iff level ≤ current profit% − `config.tp_gap_pct` (default 5) — it must sit at least one full step below current profit so it does not trigger on contact. Examples (credit $4.00): profit $1.00 = 25% ⇒ 5/10/15/20 enabled, 25+ disabled; profit $3.00 = 75% ⇒ everything up to 70 enabled. The backend re-validates the gap at execution time (profit moves between UI render and click); a violating request is rejected, never clamped.
- **TPF-03 Bot-monitored, never broker-resting.** A spread-profit floor is not expressible as a resting order; monitoring runs bot-side on every valid quote evaluation, requiring `config.tp_confirmation_evals` (default 2) consecutive breaches to filter bad prints. Stale marks (DAT-02) pause evaluation — no trigger on stale data. **The UI MUST state that the floor is active only while the bot is running** (UI-15) — the deliberate contrast with the broker-resting disaster stops.
- **TPF-04 Close procedure.** On trigger, invoke the canonical close procedure (CLS-01) with initiator `take_profit`. TPF has no close logic of its own (CLS-02).
- **TPF-05 Scope.** The floor applies to the entry's remaining open sides; already-stopped/closed sides contribute their realized P&L to profit% but are not re-traded.
- **TPF-06 Operator control.** The floor may be raised, lowered (gap rule always enforced), or cleared at any time. All changes and triggers are evented and tagged in reports like manual actions (UC-08 tagging).
- **TPF-07 No auto-trailing.** The floor never moves on its own. Automatic trailing is a future spec amendment, not an implementation liberty.
- **TPF-08 Persistence.** The floor survives restarts (REC-01). On recovery, if reconciled profit% ≤ floor, the close procedure triggers immediately — the realized level may be worse than the floor if the market moved while the bot was down; this is inherent to a bot-side floor and is shown in the day report.
- **TPF-09 Interactions.** Stop Trading: TPF monitoring and triggered closes continue (risk-reducing, consistent with EC-RSK-02). An executing Flatten All supersedes TPF. Near EOD: a triggered close works until the close per EOD-04, else the position settles per EOD-01.

## 6c. Decay buyback (DCY)

Shorts that have decayed to near-worthless are bought back early rather than held to expiry: locks the win, frees buying power, and removes the late-day tail where a spike re-inflates a "dead" short into a stop-out.

- **TPT-01 Take-profit target (v1.58, operator-ratified through worked-example debate).** A second, distinct take-profit mechanism alongside the TPF floor — OPPOSITE trigger direction: the floor closes when profit FALLS to it; the target closes when profit RISES to it. Operator-set per entry, % of total NET credit, grid {5..95 step 5}, default OFF. Entry profit% uses the TPF-01 definition verbatim (one definition, one evaluator). When profit% ≥ target for `config.tp_confirmation_evals` (default 2) consecutive valid evaluations: canonical close CLS-01, initiator `take_profit_target`. Stale marks pause evaluation (DAT-02). Stop Trading never blocks a target close (it blocks entries only). Floor and target MAY coexist on one entry — two exits on two edges of the profit path; UI MUST present them as distinct concepts ("target: close at +X%" vs "floor: never give back below Y%").
- **TPT-02 Placement.** Set per row at composition (pin-at-Save v1.47, durable ids v1.53, shown in the ▶ dialog), or added/changed/cleared on a LIVE entry card; all changes evented and report-tagged like manual actions (UC-08).
- **TPT-03 Mirror gap rule — reject, never act (operator ruling 1A).** On a live entry the target must sit ≥ 5 points ABOVE current profit%. A target at or below current profit is REJECTED with "target already passed — current profit X%" (pinned vector: profit 35% ⇒ 30% rejected, 40% is the lowest selectable). Never clamped, never treated as close-now — immediate exit already has its own button. At composition (no live profit) any grid value is valid.
- **TPT-04 Bot-monitored, NEVER broker-resting.** A resting whole-condor TP limit would rest buy orders on shorts that already carry resting stops — two working buy orders per leg, the exact double-fill race the v1.50 replace-based close exists to prevent. So the target is evaluated bot-side only; the UI carries the UI-15-style warning that it protects only while the bot runs.
- **TPT-05 Disarm on any stop (operator ruling 2B).** The moment any short stop FILLS (SIDE_STOPPED), the entry's target disarms PERMANENTLY (evented, shown on the card). From that point the entry acts exactly as it does today — surviving side rides under its own resting stop to expiry/EOD. Pinned vector: credit 4.00, target 5%; put stopped (−380), long recovered +30, call side closable for 20 ⇒ whole-entry profit +$30 = 7.5% ≥ 5% — and NOTHING happens: the target died when the stop filled; expected path rides to ≈ +$50 at expiry.
- **TPT-06 Armed feedback in dollars (operator-confirmed format).** On submit the card shows: "Exit armed: TP X% — closes at debit ≤ $D (keep ≥ $P)" where D = net credit × (1 − X%) and P = net credit × X%. Pinned vector: credit 4.00, target 60% ⇒ debit ≤ $1.60, keep ≥ $240. Pre-fill (composition) values are estimates from row parameters, labeled as such; on fill they recompute from the ACTUAL net fill credit (v1.52 rules).
- **TPT-07 Persistence & recovery.** Targets persist and restore per REC-07. On recovery, synthesized stop events process FIRST (TPT-05 disarm takes precedence); only then, if the entry is stop-free and profit% ≥ target, the close fires immediately (taking profit late is correct; the realized level may exceed the target — inherent to bot-side monitoring, shown in the day report, mirroring TPF-08).

- **DCY-01 Trigger.** A tracked short fires a buyback when its **ask** ≤ `config.decay_buyback_trigger` (default $0.05) for `config.decay_confirmation_evals` (default 2) consecutive valid evaluations (stale/sanity-rejected ticks reset the counter). Gates: RTH only and only while tracked shorts exist (structural — the watcher is event-driven on tracked shorts' quotes; there is nothing to run overnight); not after `config.decay_cutoff_time` (default 15:55 ET — Ash's choice: buy back almost to the close to kill the late re-inflation tail; expiry handles the final minutes free); never for entries in MANUAL mode or OWN-06-SUSPENDED, and never while a Flatten All is executing. Under **Stop Trading** the watcher CONTINUES (Ash's decision: stop-trading blocks new risk, and a buyback removes risk) — with one suspension rule: if the re-inflation guard's stop re-placement fails even once while in stop-trading mode, the decay watcher suspends for the remainder of the stop-trading state (never run optional tidying when protection machinery is misbehaving).
- **DCY-02 Procedure.** Routed through the canonical close service as a **short-only** close, initiator `decay` (no second close path — CLS-02): (1) cancel that short's resting stop, classified per ORD-08 — a *filled* outcome means it was actually a stop-out: abort the buyback and run the normal LEX path; (2) on confirmed cancel, place a limit buy-to-close at the trigger price; (3) **re-inflation guard:** if the buyback is unfilled after `config.decay_unfilled_timeout_seconds` (default 30 s) OR the ask rises above the trigger, cancel the buyback and **re-place the resting stop** — protection is restored and the buyback attempt ends. The unprotected window is bounded by this rule and carries near-zero risk (the short was at $0.05).
- **DCY-03 Leftover long.** After a decay buyback the side's long is **left to expire** — it is further OTM than the dead short, its bid is ~zero, and it acts as a free hedge. Side state: `SIDE_CLOSED_DECAY` (short realized, residual long settling per EOD-01). LEX-07's always-sell applies to stop-outs, where the long has real value — not here. The residual long still occupies its strike for STK-09.
- **DCY-04 Accounting.** Buyback fills realize that side's P&L (PNL-02); the day report lists decay closes distinctly; order-cap usage (RSK-08) is charged as normal (exit-priority, never blocked by the cap).

## 7. End of day (EOD)

- **EOD-01** Default: all remaining positions are held to expiration. SPX 0DTE is cash-settled to SET/closing value; no exercise/assignment handling is required. After settlement, the bot marks all remaining sides `EXPIRED` and computes final P&L.
- **EOD-02** If `config.eod_close_time` is set (e.g. 15:55 ET), the bot closes all open entries via the canonical close procedure (CLS-01, initiator `eod`), with a marketable fallback deadline at `config.eod_close_deadline`.
- **EOD-03** All resting stop orders for positions that expired or were closed MUST be cancelled at EOD; the day does not end until the bot confirms zero working orders remain (or logs a critical alert naming each one it could not cancel).
- **EOD-04** An in-flight LEX procedure at market close: keep working until close; whatever remains expires per EOD-01.
- **EOD-05** After EOD the bot produces a day report: per-entry credits, stops hit, long-sale recoveries, fees, realized P&L, and every rule-triggered skip/abort with its reason.

## 8. Risk and safety (RSK)

- **RSK-01 Stop Trading.** A persistent operator toggle (UI + CLI + API) that blocks all new entries immediately — **and does nothing else**: existing positions, resting stops, LEX, TPF and the decay watcher all continue unchanged. State persists across restarts and requires explicit operator reset ("Resume trading"). Orthogonal to Flatten All (RSK-01a): flatten closes the book, Stop Trading stops refilling it; a full emergency stop is both together.
- **RSK-01a Flatten all.** A **one-shot action** that closes the book and nothing more: (1) cancels all working entry orders (CLS-03 per entry); (2) closes every entry with open sides via the canonical close procedure (CLS-01, initiator `manual_flatten`), entries processed concurrently with exit-priority rate limiting (EC-API-02); armed TPF floors are cleared; in-flight LEX ladders are superseded by immediate marketable-limit closes (EC-RSK-02). **It does NOT block future entries** (Ash's rule): if Stop Trading is inactive, the next scheduled entry fires normally into the clean book. While the flatten is executing, entry attempts are skipped (`flatten_in_progress`). The confirmation dialog offers an "Also enable Stop Trading" checkbox (default unchecked — flatten is purely a close).
- **RSK-01b Stop Trading & Flatten (combined control).** A single UI button ("Stop Trading & Flatten") that executes both paths with one action, in this order: (1) activate Stop Trading (RSK-01) — entries are blocked before any close begins; (2) execute Flatten All (RSK-01a). Not a third code path: it invokes exactly the two existing controls. Same typed-FLATTEN confirmation as RSK-01a. This is the full emergency stop.
- **RSK-02 — REMOVED (v1.32, Ash's decision). MUST NOT BE BUILT.** There is no daily loss limit, no `daily_max_loss`, no automatic loss-triggered halt or flatten, and no `daily_loss` close initiator. The risk model is: per-position broker-resting stops + operator-composed entry count at arm time + the operator's Stop Trading / Flatten controls. Deliberate, eyes-open consequence: on a cascade day the bot runs the full composed schedule unless the operator intervenes. Any future re-introduction is a spec amendment, not an implementation choice.
- **RSK-03 Position reconciliation gate.** Trading is blocked whenever internal state and broker state **genuinely disagree** (see REC-02) until reconciled. **Genuine mismatch (v1.49) is exactly:** (a) a bot-owned symbol whose broker quantity is LESS than the ledger (shortfall — someone closed the bot's lots), (b) a bot order ID or fill the broker reports that the event log cannot account for, or (c) attribution impossible (order-ID data unavailable). A position fully explained as FOREIGN by OWN-02 attribution is NOT a mismatch and does not trip this gate (OWN-03).
- **RSK-04 Max exposure.** New entries are blocked if worst-case loss of open condors + the new one exceeds `config.max_day_risk`. **Scope (v1.49, operator-ratified): the ceiling caps the BOT'S OWN book only** — foreign positions are excluded (the bot cannot price a strategy it doesn't know and must not guess, OWN-03); the broker's buying-power gate is where the foreign book naturally constrains new entries. The UI banner MUST state that max_day_risk covers bot-placed risk only. Since contracts are per-entry (ENT-04, v1.44), the day's exposure is the **SUM of per-entry worst cases** — worst case per entry = (its wing width − its net credit) × 100 × its contracts, per side not both, since only one side can settle in the money — never n × a global size. Both sides can *stop*, so realized whipsaw risk uses stop distances; document both numbers in the UI, per row and as a day total.
- **RSK-05 Fat-finger guards.** Reject internally any order whose price is > `config.sanity_price_multiple` × current mid, or whose quantity ≠ expected entry quantity, before it reaches the broker.
- **RSK-06 Alerting.** Critical alerts (unprotected position, unfilled fallback, reconciliation mismatch, Stop Trading activated, Flatten All executed, daily loss breached, repeated API failures) are pushed via `config.alert_channels` and surfaced in the UI banner. An unacknowledged critical alert does not block resting-stop protection (which is broker-side by design) but blocks new entries if `config.block_entries_on_critical` (default true).
- **RSK-07 Clock discipline.** Clock drift beyond `config.max_clock_drift_ms` blocks new entries (resting stops are unaffected — another reason they live at the broker).
- **RSK-08 Daily order-count rail.** Total orders submitted per day (including cancel/replaces — each replace counts as a new order) must stay under `config.daily_order_cap` (default 380, below Cboe's 390/day professional-customer threshold, which is assessed as a monthly average of orders per day). When the cap is near (`config.order_cap_buffer` remaining), new entries are blocked; exit-side orders (stops, LEX, flatten) are NEVER blocked by the cap.

## 9. Market data (DAT)

- **DAT-01** Quotes and greeks stream via DXLink; REST snapshots are for startup/reconciliation only.
- **DAT-02** A quote is *stale* if older than `config.max_quote_age_ms`. Decisions (strike selection, ladders, floors) MUST NOT be made on stale data; the specific rule's fallback applies.
- **DAT-03** Stream disconnect: reconnect with exponential backoff. While disconnected: no new entries, no ladder repricing (existing working limits remain); resting stops continue protecting shorts.
- **DAT-04** Trading-halt detection (limit up/down, exchange halt): treat as DAT-03 plus block entry attempts. On resume, do not "catch up" missed entries (ENT-02).
- **DAT-05** Underlying spot for intrinsic (LEX-04) uses the SPX index feed, same staleness rule.

## 10. Recovery and reconciliation (REC)

- **REC-01** All state transitions are persisted as an append-only event log before being acted on (event-sourced core — doc 05). The bot can rebuild in-memory state from the log at any time.
- **REC-02** On startup or reconnect, the bot fetches broker truth (positions, working orders, fills since last event) and reconciles. **Broker is authoritative for positions and fills; the event log is authoritative for intent.** Mismatches: bot adopts broker positions, logs a `ReconciliationMismatch`, and applies RSK-03.
- **REC-03** Startup mid-day: resume management of any live positions found (re-attach to resting stops, resume LEX ladders for stopped sides whose long is still held, resume EOD schedule). Missed entry times are skipped (ENT-02).
- **REC-04** If the bot finds a short position with **no resting stop**, it checks in order: (1) did the stop fill? → run LEX (EC-STP-06); (2) was the stop cancelled by someone other than the bot? → operator intent, do NOT re-place (OWN-11, `USER_UNPROTECTED` + alert + Re-protect button); (3) otherwise (never confirmed, crash before placement, terminal artifact) → `UNPROTECTED` (STP-04), re-place immediately.
- **REC-05** Duplicate-order prevention across restarts relies on ORD-04 idempotency keys; on startup, the bot checks broker orders for its keys before re-submitting anything.
- **REC-07 Persistent-state inventory.** The following are durable state: written to the persistent store on every change, restored EXACTLY on every boot (process restart, crash, Docker container recovery, machine reboot), and never reset by time or restart. Safe defaults apply on fresh install only. The inventory: (1) ARMED/DISARMED (ENT-01a); (2) Stop Trading (RSK-01); (3) Confirm Live (ENT-01b); (4) `trading_mode` paper/live (DAY-05); (5) the standing entry schedule and all per-entry parameters; (6) the active config version (all settings); (7) armed TPF floors (TPF-08); (8) the event log and all entry/position state (REC-01); (9) OWN ledger and SUSPENDED/quarantine states; (10) paper-mode cash ledger and simulated positions. A state not on this list must not silently accumulate persistence assumptions — extending the inventory is a spec amendment. TC-ENT-07's Docker scenario asserts the inventory survives container recovery.
- **REC-06** tastytrade session/token expiry is renewed proactively; auth failure triggers DAT-03-style behaviour plus a critical alert.

## 10a. Position ownership boundary (OWN)

The broker nets positions by symbol; closing orders draw on the netted pool by the broker's lot rules. Order IDs identify orders, not lots — no software can guarantee it closed "the bot's specific contract" when bot and operator hold the same symbol. What the bot CAN guarantee, and MUST: it never transacts more than its own recorded quantity, and it refuses and alerts the moment the account stops matching its books.

- **OWN-01 Ownership ledger.** The bot maintains a per-symbol owned-quantity ledger built **exclusively from fills on its own order IDs** (event log). Operator/manual trades never enter the ledger, its P&L, or its risk marks (buying-power checks use broker reality; everything else uses the ledger).
- **OWN-02 Foreign detection.** `foreign_delta(symbol) = broker net position − bot ledger`, recomputed at every reconciliation and on every position-update event.
- **OWN-03 FOREIGN quarantine — never touch, never block (v1.49, operator-ratified).** Any position or delta not attributable to the bot's own fills is FOREIGN: the bot never places stops on it, never closes it, never hedges it, never includes it in P&L. Detection raises a critical alert and a persistent UI banner. **FOREIGN is an EXPLAINED state, not a mismatch: its presence alone MUST NOT block arming or entries** — the bot trades alongside the operator's existing book (single-account operation is a first-class mode), subject to STK-09's foreign-occupancy rule and the ENT-03 buying-power gate against broker reality (which already carries the foreign book's margin). **This includes a foreign naked short** — alert-only; the bot must not guess operator intent (a stop it places could dismantle a deliberate hedge). Distinguish from crash orphans: a position that DOES match the bot's own recorded fills (by order ID) is adopted and managed per REC-03/04 as before.
- **OWN-04 Quantity cap — structural.** Every exit-side order (stop, LEX, CLS, flatten) is capped at the ledger quantity for that symbol at submit time. Enforced in the single order-construction path, not per call site.
- **OWN-05 Shared symbol — constrain, warn, keep managing.** When foreign_delta ≠ 0 on a bot-held symbol (operator traded a strike the bot holds): automated management continues at recorded ledger quantities (stops keep resting, LEX and closes proceed, all OWN-04-capped); a persistent warning states that broker lot-matching is now ambiguous and that the only full isolation is not sharing symbols. The bot never suspends risk-reducing work over a shared symbol.
- **OWN-06 Ledger shortfall.** If the broker shows LESS than the ledger (operator manually closed lots the bot placed): **SUSPEND** that entry's automation (state `SUSPENDED`), write down the ledger to broker truth with a `ForeignReduction` event, critical alert, and require operator acknowledgment before automation resumes on that entry. Never fire compensating orders automatically.
- **OWN-09 External close — detect and stand down.** A tracked short absent from broker positions whose stop order did NOT fill is an external close. Guards against false positives (all required): (a) the position was previously observed open in the positions feed (`seen_open`); (b) at least `config.external_close_grace_seconds` (default 60) since stop placement — position feeds lag fresh fills; (c) confirmed on two consecutive reconcile passes; (d) fill events on the order stream always outrank position-feed absence. On detection: stand down all automation for that side (monitoring, LEX, TPF, EOD close), mark the side `CLOSED_EXTERNAL`, and **touch no orders — including the bot's own leftover stop** (operator rule: after a manual intervention, ALL order cleanup belongs to the operator). The critical alert lists every bot order still working with its precise consequence spelled out (e.g. "stop BUY 2 × 5990P still resting: if triggered against your now-flat book it will OPEN a long position") and carries one-click operator-initiated actions (**Cancel stop**). A stop that shows FILLED is a normal stop-out and routes to LEX — never mislabeled external.
- **OWN-10 Partial external reduction.** Broker quantity below ledger but above zero (operator closed part of the bot's position): suspend per OWN-06, write down the ledger, and **take no order action** — the now-oversized stop is the operator's to fix (their rule: the bot never fixes the consequences of manual interventions). The critical alert spells out the over-buy chain explicitly ("your stop buys 2; only 1 short remains; a trigger will close 1 and OPEN 1 long at that strike") and carries one-click operator-initiated actions: **Resize stop to remaining quantity** and **Cancel stop**. The buttons are operator actions — clicking is deciding; the bot merely executes.
- **OWN-11 Operator actions at the broker are never overridden.** A bot order found cancelled where the cancel was NOT initiated by the bot is treated as operator intent and is **never re-placed automatically**. The affected side is marked `USER_UNPROTECTED`; a critical alert states plainly: "this short has no stop and the bot will not restore it"; the UI banner carries a one-click **Re-protect** action (operator-initiated re-placement). Automatic re-placement (STP-04, REC-04) applies ONLY to bot-side failures: placement never confirmed, crash before placement, terminal expiry artifacts (ORD-08b). Residual risk, accepted deliberately: a broker-side cancel (ops purge, exchange action) is indistinguishable from an operator cancel and will also not be re-placed — the alert covers both.
- **OWN-07 No account-level endpoints.** The bot never calls account-wide flatten/close-all APIs. Flatten-all (RSK-01a) iterates the bot's own entries at recorded quantities, nothing else.
- **OWN-08 Prevention beats detection.** STK-09 already prevents the bot from opening on any occupied strike (bot-initiated sharing impossible). Operator-initiated sharing cannot be prevented, only detected (OWN-02/05); the UI surfaces the recommendation to avoid trading the bot's strikes manually.

## 11. Fees and P&L (PNL)

- **PNL-01** All P&L calculations include per-contract commissions and fees from `config.fee_model` (SPX has index option fees; keep the model configurable and reconcilable against broker statements).
- **PNL-02** Per-entry P&L = entry credit − stop buy-backs + long-sale proceeds − remaining-leg settlement effects − fees. The UI shows per-entry, per-side and per-day figures, live.
- **PNL-03** Live day P&L (UI + TPF evaluation) = realized + conservative mark of open positions (mid, falling back to worst of bid/ask when stale). Intraday figures are always bot-computed from own fills — deterministic, immediate, replayable (REC-01); TPF never waits on broker reporting.
- **PNL-04 Broker transaction history is authoritative for the permanent record.** At EOD (and on demand), a reconciliation pass pulls tastytrade transaction history, recomputes per-entry realized P&L from it, and the **broker-derived figure is what the day report and all stored history present as authoritative**. Divergence from the bot-computed figure above `config.pnl_reconcile_tolerance` (default $0.05 per entry) raises a `PnlMismatch` flag in the report and an alert; systematic divergences feed corrections back into the fee model (PNL-01) so drift is fixed at the source. Rationale (predecessor system, production): fees, leg-fill allocations and settlement values make self-computed realized P&L drift from what actually hits the account; the broker's ledger wins every dispute.

## 12. Paper-mode fill simulation (SIM)

Paper mode is a **fill simulator fed by real market data** — never tastytrade's cert sandbox, whose unconditional instant fills make economics meaningless (cert is used only for API contract tests). The point of SIM: paper P&L must be a defensible, conservative estimate of live P&L.

- **SIM-01 Wiring.** In paper mode the `BrokerGateway` port is bound to a `SimulatedBroker` adapter at the composition root; the live adapter is never constructed (EC-RSK-04). Market data is the REAL production DXLink feed. Every other component — gates, selection, ladders, stops, LEX, TPF, DCY, OWN, event log, reports — runs identically and unaware of the mode.
- **SIM-02 Limit-fill model (trade-through, not touch).** A simulated limit order fills only when it is genuinely executable against the real market: (a) the spread's NATURAL price (sell legs valued at bid, buy legs at ask) satisfies the limit, OR (b) the net mid beats the limit by ≥ `config.sim_fill_through_ticks` (default 1). Mid merely touching the limit does NOT fill — touch-equals-fill systematically flatters paper results. Fills are all-or-nothing per contract; per-leg fill prices are allocated from the quotes at fill time (mirroring the live allocation caveat).
- **SIM-03 Stop simulation.** Simulated resting stops trigger when the short's `config.sim_trigger_source` (default `mark`; align with the live trigger source once STP-05a verification establishes it) reaches the trigger; fill price = trigger + `config.sim_stop_slippage_ticks` (default 3) — stop-markets never fill at the trigger in real life, and paper must not pretend otherwise. Slippage is recorded exactly as live (EC-STP-03), feeding the same calibration records. Honesty note: in paper the "resting" stop lives in-process — paper can never demonstrate bot-independent protection; that proof belongs to the sandbox drill (UC-12/TC-STP-08).
- **SIM-04 Money.** A simulated cash ledger starts at `config.sim_starting_cash` (default $100,000). Every fill posts ±(price × 100 × contracts); every leg pays the full fee model (PNL-01). Margin: each open entry consumes buying power per the SPX spread requirement (max of the two sides' (width − credit) × 100 × contracts), released on close — so the ENT-03 buying-power gate and RSK-04 strain against simulated capital exactly as live. 0DTE positions cash-settle against the real SPX closing level, posting to the ledger like real expiry. The ledger is durable state (REC-07).
- **SIM-05 Pipeline identity.** Simulated fills emit the same order events as live; the event log, OWN ledger, NLE calibration, TPF evaluation and PNL-04 reconciliation (run against the simulator's own transaction ledger) all execute the identical code paths. Every report and record is stamped PAPER (UI-12) — paper history can never masquerade as live.
- **SIM-06 Honesty surface.** What paper cannot simulate is displayed, not hidden (UI-12 tooltip): no queue position, no partial-fill dynamics (all-or-nothing), modeled rather than real stop slippage, and no proof of broker-side stop independence. The pessimism knobs default conservative so that live performance should meet or beat paper expectations — never the reverse.
